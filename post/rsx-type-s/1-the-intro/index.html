I Bought an RSX Type S With a Blown Transmission

RSX Type-S
Aug 30, 2025

About a month back I was staying at an AirBnB with some buddies for an autocross event when one of them casually mentioned they had a friend with a RSX Type S that had a (potentially) blown transmission just sitting around. They weren’t exactly sure what was wrong with it but the owner wasn’t mechanically inclined and was just looking to get rid of it.

I wasn’t actually in the market for another car but it was too good of an opportunity to pass up and a week later it was sitting in my driveway.

She’s a little rough on the edges and has 318k but for the most part it all checks out. It’s got the typical rust in the back quarters and some more under the hood.

The driver door took a hard ding at some point.

And the driver fender has paint cracking from a bad repair.

But it already has some nice mods like being lowered on Function & Form Type 1 coil overs. I believe the spring rates are 8k front, and 10k rear which is basically perfect for autocross.

Why Is the Transmission Locked Up?

My first idea for the RSX was to part it out and use it as a donor car for K-swapping my EG hatch but after looking it over I think the RSX can still be made road worthy and have some fun with auto crossing it. I’m also not looking to K-swap my EG anytime soon so even if I did use it as a donor it wouldn’t be for a few years at best which means it’d just sit in my yard.

Before I could make any solid plans with it I needed to figure out what was going on with the transmission. Right now the RSX won’t move and if you try and shift it in gear and let the clutch out it just stalls. The clutch itself feels decent and based on what the previous owner told me about how the front wheels locked up on him while driving I didn’t expect I’d get lucky and find a bad clutch.

After jacking up the front of the car and putting it on jack stands I started off by draining the transmission oil to see if it was full of glitter or metal bits but instead I found almost nothing.

That’s all the fluid that came out of the transmission. There was maybe 3-4 ounces in it when OE spec is 1.8qts. Not good.

Armed with my PB blaster and a print out of some FSM pages I began pulling the transmission.

This is my first K series car so it was a nice treat unhooking the shifter linkage from atop the transmission instead of trying to press out the bitch pin that B series transmissions have.

Missing Some Bolts

Whoever had the transmission out last saved me some time by skimping on some of the bolts.

Subframe Is Out And The Transmission Is Free

After a few hours of wrenching I was able to get the subframe dropped and popped the transmission off the engine. For the most part this went fairly easy other than a couple stuck bolts.

Found Some Cool Go Fast Parts

As I was pulling parts I couldn’t help but notice the RSX already has some pretty cool things on it. It’s got Hasport engine mounts.

An Injen cold air intake / washer reservoir.

An Ebay brand clutch. (Okay maybe not cool)

And even a Skunk2 header + exhaust system.

The exhaust is super loud and missing it’s cat so I’ll need to address that.
